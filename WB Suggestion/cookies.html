Cookie Usage

We use digital cookies. Sadly, you cannot eat them.

1. What are Cookies?

Cookies (and Local Storage) are small pieces of text sent to your browser by a website you visit. They help the site remember information about your visit, which makes it easier to visit the site again and makes the site more useful to you.

At Wolf Blood Workshop, we keep it simple. We don't use invasive tracking cookies to sell your data to the highest bidder.

2. The Cookie Jar (What we store)

We primarily use Local Storage, which stays on your device and isn't sent to our servers. Here is exactly what we store:

Name Type Purpose
wb_theme_pref Local Storage Remembers if you prefer Dark Mode (Default) or... well, there is only Dark Mode.
wb_read_roles Local Storage Remembers which "New" roles you have clicked on so the notification dot disappears.
wb_votes Local Storage Prevents you from voting on the same role multiple times (Basic spam protection).

3. Third-Party Services

We use some external tools that might place their own cookies. We don't control these, but they are standard for the web:

  • Loader Animation (Three.js): Loads graphics libraries from CDNs (Content Delivery Networks) which may cache files for faster loading.
  • Telegram Links: Clicking "Join the Pack" takes you to Telegram. They have their own cookie policy, which is probably much longer than ours.

4. How to Manage Cookies

If you really hate these helpful little files, you can delete them.

Go to your browser settings (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari) -> Privacy & Security -> Clear Browsing Data. Select "Cookies and other site data."

Warning: If you do this, the site might forget which roles you've already voted for.
